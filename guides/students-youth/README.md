# Students & Youth

Below are some ideas for quick guides. If you’ve found a way to stay safe online at school or on social media, feel free to add it to the list!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **The "Permanent Record" is Real:** How to clean up your social media footprint before applying for colleges or jobs.
* **Game Mods & "Cracked" Software:** Why those "free" game cheats often come with hidden miners or password stealers.
* **Sextortion Awareness:** What to do if someone threatens to share private photos—and why you should never pay them.
* **Safe AI Use:** How to use tools like ChatGPT without accidentally feeding them your personal data or private homework.
* **Public School Wi-Fi:** What the school can (and can't) see when you're on their network and how to stay private.
* **The "Scholarship" Scam:** How to spot fake financial aid offers that ask for an "application fee" or your bank info.
* **Edu-Phishing:** Recognizing emails that look like they’re from your University Portal but are actually trying to steal your login.
* **Privacy on Campus Apps:** Auditing the permissions of apps you're forced to download for school or clubs.
* **Oversharing on Social Media:** Why posting your school ID, dorm room number, or class schedule is a bad idea.
* **Protecting your "Digital Reputation":** How to handle online drama or cyberbullying without letting it escalate.
* **Safe Reselling:** Avoiding scams when buying or selling textbooks and tech on Discord or Facebook Marketplace.
* **Two-Factor for Everything:** Why you should secure your Discord, Steam, and Instagram accounts just as much as your bank.
* **The "Internship" Trap:** Spotting fake LinkedIn recruiters who offer "remote internships" just to get your ID documents.
* **Secure Notes:** Keeping your research and papers safe from being stolen or "leaked" before they are turned in.

This list is for you! If you have a topic that matters to students today, turn it into a guide or suggest a new idea.
