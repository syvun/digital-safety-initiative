# Survivors of Domestic Violence

Digital safety in this category isn't just about privacy—it's about physical safety. These guides are meant to help anyone regain control over their devices, accounts, and location. 

This list is a starting point for potential guides. If you see a gap or have a new idea that could help someone stay safe, please add it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **The "Spy in Your Pocket":** How to find and remove hidden stalkerware or parental control apps used for tracking.
* **Smart Home Lockdown:** Securing (or disabling) shared cameras, smart locks, and thermostats that an abuser might still access.
* **Car Tracking 101:** Checking for GPS trackers or built-in car apps (like OnStar or FordPass) that share your location.
* **The "Panic" Plan:** How to quickly wipe your browser history or lock your phone in an emergency.
* **Hidden Digital Lives:** Creating a "safe" email or social media account that isn't linked to any old phone numbers or backup emails.
* **Ghosting your Location:** Managing "Find My Friends," Snapchat Maps, and Google Maps Timeline settings.
* **Safe Communication:** Why Signal or Telegram with disappearing messages might be better than regular texting.
* **Shared Account Audit:** How to safely leave a family plan (iCloud, Google Family, Netflix) without alerting the other person.
* **Social Media Stealth Mode:** Locking down privacy settings so your posts don't reveal your current city or workplace.
* **Metadata Warnings:** How to stop your photos from "leaking" your exact GPS coordinates when you send them.
* **Emergency Tech Kit:** What devices and cables to take with you if you have to leave quickly.
* **Password Resets & Security Questions:** Changing answers to things only an abuser would know (like your mother’s maiden name).
* **Public Record Privacy:** How to keep your new address out of online databases and voter registration sites.
* **Safe Devices:** When it's better to buy a "burner" phone versus trying to clean an old, compromised one.
* **App Permission Audit:** Checking which apps have access to your microphone, camera, and location for no reason.

This is an open list. If you have a specific tip on how to untangle a digital life from an abuser, feel free to turn it into a guide or suggest a new topic.
