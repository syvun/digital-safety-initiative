# Veterans & Military Families

Below are some ideas for guides tailored to the military community. If you have a tip that helped your family stay safe, feel free to add it to the list!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Affinity Fraud:** How to spot scammers who pretend to be "fellow veterans" or use military lingo to gain your trust.
* **Securing VA Portals:** Best practices for keeping your My HealtheVet and other VA.gov accounts locked down.
* **OPSEC for Families:** Why posting "Welcome Home" countdowns or specific locations during deployment can be a safety risk.
* **Protecting the DD-214:** Why you should never upload your discharge papers to unverified sites or "veteran discount" databases.
* **The "Deployment Mode" Checklist:** How to secure your home Wi-Fi and smart devices before heading overseas.
* **Scam-Proofing Veteran Benefits:** Identifying fake calls about "updated" disability ratings or "pre-approved" VA loans.
* **Romance Scams (Military Edition):** How to help friends or family spot scammers who steal photos of service members to trick people online.
* **Military Star Card Safety:** Protecting your credit and account info from phishing attempts targeting service-connected credit lines.
* **USAID & Non-Profit Scams:** How to verify if a "veteran charity" is real before donating your money or data.
* **Safe Job Hunting for Transitioning Members:** Spotting fake recruiters who target veterans specifically to harvest their security clearance info.
* **Managing "Space-A" and Travel Privacy:** Keeping your travel plans private when using military transport or lodging.
* **Securing Military Email (OSINT):** How to minimize what someone can find out about you just by searching your name or military email.

This list is open for anyone in the community. If you have a practical tip for military digital resilience, we want to see it turned into a guide!
