# Immigrants & Refugees

This folder is all about staying safe while navigating a new life. Digital safety is a huge part of protecting your legal status and your future. 

Below is a broad list of topics we want to cover. This is just to get us started—if you have a better idea or a specific situation in mind, definitely go for it and add it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Spotting "Ghost" Lawyers:** How to check if a legal aid website or "notario" is a scam.
* **The ".gov" Rule:** A quick way to tell real government emails (USCIS, IRS) from fake ones.
* **Safe Money:** How to use Zelle, Remitly, or Wise without getting your account flagged or scammed.
* **Digital Documents:** Where and how to keep photos of your passport and visa so you don't lose them if your phone breaks.
* **Phony Job Offers:** Warning signs of "cash only" jobs that are actually just harvesting your ID data.
* **Phone Privacy at Borders:** What you should know about your digital rights and your devices.
* **Encrypted Family Chat:** Moving your family group chats to safer apps like Signal or WhatsApp.
* **Bond Payment Scams:** What to do if you get a scary call saying a relative is detained and you need to pay "now."
* **Public Wi-Fi for Paperwork:** How to safely log into sensitive accounts if you're using library or shelter Wi-Fi.
* **Protecting your SSN/ITIN:** Who actually needs your number and who is just fishing for it.
* **Social Media & Immigration:** Why privacy settings matter when your status is being processed.
* **Fake "Grant" Apps:** Avoiding apps that promise "free government money" for newcomers.
* **Translating Safely:** Using translation tools without accidentally uploading sensitive personal letters to public servers.
* **Address Privacy:** How to manage your physical address in public records if you're worried about safety.

This list is just the beginning. Feel free to grab a topic and turn it into a simple, one-page guide, or come up with something completely new!
