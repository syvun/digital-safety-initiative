# Rural Residents

Living in a rural area often means dealing with slower internet, satellite connections, and unique types of local scams. This folder is about keeping your digital life secure, even when you're far from the city.

Below is a broad list of topics we want to cover. If you have a tip for staying safe in a small town or on a farm, feel free to add your ideas!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Securing "Smart Farming" Tech:** How to protect your soil sensors, drones, and automated tractors from being hacked or accessed by others.
* **The "Satellite Internet" Audit:** Simple ways to secure Starlink or other satellite routers so your neighbors (or strangers) aren't using your bandwidth.
* **Emergency Offline Communication:** Setting up "Mesh" networks or walkie-talkie apps for when the internet or cell towers go down.
* **Land & Mineral Rights Scams:** Spotting fake emails or letters from "investors" trying to trick you into signing away your property rights.
* **Low-Bandwidth Security:** How to keep your devices updated and secure without eating up your entire monthly data cap in one day.
* **The "Local" Phishing Trap:** Recognizing scams that use the names of local businesses, banks, or utilities to seem more trustworthy.
* **Secure Home Networks on Large Properties:** How to safely extend your Wi-Fi to barns or workshops without creating security holes.
* **Ranch & Property Surveillance:** Setting up outdoor cameras that respect privacy while keeping your equipment and livestock safe.
* **Avoiding "Rural Grant" Scams:** Identifying fake offers for government subsidies or farming equipment grants that ask for upfront fees.
* **The "Service Repair" Scam:** What to do when someone calls claiming your well pump, tractor, or solar system has a "digital error" they need to fix remotely.
* **Protecting Community Groups:** Staying safe in local Facebook groups or "Nextdoor" without oversharing your daily routine or when you’re away from the house.
* **Backing Up in the Bush:** Why physical backups (like external hard drives) are a must when cloud uploads are too slow or unreliable.

This list is just a starting point. If you have a practical tip for staying digitally resilient in a rural setting, we’d love for you to turn it into a guide!
