# Victims of Identity Theft

This is a broad list of ideas for recovery and protection. If you’ve been through this and have a tip that saved you, please share it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **The "Burn It Down" Checklist:** A step-by-step list of who to call first (banks, credit bureaus, police).
* **Freezing Your Credit:** How to lock your credit reports so no one can open a new card in your name (and how to "unfreeze" it later).
* **The Session Audit:** How to see every device currently logged into your Google, Facebook, or Bank account—and how to kick the scammers out.
* **Security Question Makeover:** Why you should use fake answers for "What is your mother's maiden name?" (because scammers already know the real one).
* **Handling a "Tax Identity" Theft:** What to do if someone already filed a tax return in your name to steal your refund.
* **The Social Media Recovery Plan:** How to get your account back if you’ve been locked out by a hacker.
* **Mailing Address Hijacking:** How to check if someone is redirecting your physical mail to steal your bills and bank statements.
* **"My ID was used for a crime":** What to do if someone committed a crime or got a ticket using your name.
* **Protecting your SSN:** A guide on who actually has a legal right to ask for your Social Security Number and when to say no.
* **Checking for "Dark Web" Leaks:** Simple (and free) ways to see if your passwords or emails are being sold online.
* **The Clean Email Strategy:** Setting up a fresh, "clean" email address just for banking and recovery, separate from your everyday mail.
* **Identity Theft Affidavit:** How to fill out the FTC reports so you have a legal paper trail.
* **Medical Identity Theft:** Spotting fake charges on your medical bills or insurance and how to fix your records.
* **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Upgrade:** Moving from SMS codes (which can be intercepted) to Authenticator apps.

This list is always evolving. If you have a specific step for recovering from identity theft, feel free to turn it into a guide or suggest a new topic!
