# Senior Citizens

Digital safety for seniors is all about staying connected while keeping the scammers out. We want to make technology feel safe and helpful, not confusing or dangerous.

This list is full of ideas for short, friendly guides. If you have a tip that would make life easier for a grandparent or an older neighbor, definitely add it to the list!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **The "Grandparent" Scam:** What to do if someone calls pretending to be a grandchild in trouble (and how to spot AI voice cloning).
* **The "Friendly" Stranger:** Recognizing "Romance Scams" on Facebook or dating sites where people ask for money or gift cards.
* **Fake Virus Alerts:** How to handle those scary "Your computer is infected" pop-ups without calling the fake support number.
* **Medicare & Social Security Safety:** How to tell a real government call from a scammer trying to steal your benefits.
* **The Magic of "Legacy Contacts":** Setting up someone you trust to manage your digital accounts if something happens to you.
* **Safe Online Shopping:** How to tell if a website is a real store or just a fake page designed to steal your credit card.
* **Password Management for Humans:** Simple ways to keep track of passwords without using the same one for everything.
* **The "Check First" Rule:** A simple habit of calling a trusted family member before clicking any "urgent" links in an email.
* **Social Media Privacy:** Making sure your Facebook posts (with info about your house or travel) are only seen by real friends.
* **Banking Alert Setup:** How to turn on "Large Transaction" alerts so you know the second money moves from your account.
* **QR Code Safety:** Why you shouldn't scan every random barcode you see at the bus stop or in a restaurant.
* **Tablet & Phone Hardening:** Simple settings to make devices easier to use and harder to hack (like larger fonts and auto-updates).
* **Sweepstakes & Lottery Scams:** Why you never have to pay a "processing fee" or "taxes" to claim a prize you didn't enter for.
* **Sharing Photos Safely:** Using shared albums instead of posting everything publicly where scammers can harvest family info.

This is a living list. If you have a simple way to explain a complex security tip to a senior, we’d love to see it turned into a guide!
