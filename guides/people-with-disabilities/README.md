# People with Disabilities

This list is a starting point. If you have an idea for making security easier to navigate for people with different accessibility needs, please add it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Medical IoT Safety:** How to secure "smart" medical devices like pacemakers, insulin pumps, or CPAP machines from unauthorized access.
* **Voice Assistant Privacy:** Managing Alexa, Siri, or Google Home settings to prevent them from "over-listening" or making accidental purchases.
* **Accessible 2FA:** Finding the best Multi-Factor Authentication methods for your needs (like using hardware keys instead of typing small codes).
* **"Service Animal" Scams:** How to spot fake registration websites that sell "official" certificates or vests that aren't legally required.
* **Safe Assistive Tech:** Why you should be careful when downloading unofficial accessibility apps or "screen readers" from unknown sources.
* **Protecting Health Data:** Tips for keeping your medical records and insurance info private when using online portals or apps.
* **Identifying "Disability Grant" Scams:** Spotting fake offers for government funding or specialized equipment that ask for an upfront fee.
* **Simplified Security Dashboards:** How to set up easy-to-read privacy settings that aren't buried in complex menus.
* **Screen Reader Privacy:** Making sure your device doesn't read your passwords or private info out loud in public spaces.
* **The "Emergency Contact" Setup:** How to quickly share your location or medical ID with trusted people in case of a crisis.
* **Safe Remote Work for Disabled Professionals:** Securing your home setup when working for companies that might require specific tracking software.
* **Avoiding Fraudulent "Cures":** Recognizing predatory ads and phishing emails that promise "miracle" treatments in exchange for personal data.

This is an open list for the community. If you have a tip that makes digital safety more inclusive, we’d love for you to turn it into a guide!
