# "Digital Nomads" by Necessity

This is a list of ideas to help anyone stay secure while "living out of a backpack." If you have a tip for staying safe on the go, please add it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Public Wi-Fi Survival:** How to safely use shelter, library, or coffee shop Wi-Fi without letting others see what you’re doing.
* **The "Juice Jacking" Trap:** Why you should use a "USB data blocker" (or just a wall outlet) instead of random USB charging ports in public places.
* **Physical Device Security:** Simple ways to encrypt your phone and laptop so your data stays private even if the device is lost or stolen.
* **Virtual Mailboxes:** How to get a digital address for your paperwork so you don't have to share your actual physical location.
* **Cold Storage for Essentials:** Using an encrypted USB drive to keep scans of your ID and birth certificate, even if you lose internet access.
* **The "Find My Device" Setup:** Making sure you can track, lock, or wipe your phone from any computer if it disappears.
* **Managing "Shared" Computers:** A checklist for using library computers—like how to make sure you’re truly logged out and your history is gone.
* **Battery & Data Management:** How to keep your phone alive and your data usage low when you don't know when your next charge will be.
* **Offline Maps & Safety:** How to download maps and emergency info so you can find your way without a signal.
* **Multi-Factor Authentication (2FA) without a Phone Number:** What to do if you lose your SIM card or change numbers often (using backup codes or apps).
* **Privacy in Shelters:** Tips for keeping your screen private and your passwords hidden when you’re living in close quarters with others.
* **Scam-Proofing "Free" Help:** How to tell if a website offering "free beds" or "free meals" is just a scam to get your personal info.

This is an open list. If you’ve found a way to stay digitally secure while navigating housing instability, your advice could really help someone else!
