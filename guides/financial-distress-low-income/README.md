# People in Financial Distress & Low-Income Families

This list is a starting point for practical guides. If you know a trick to save money safely or protect government aid, please add it!

### Ideas for short guides:

* **Protecting your EBT Card:** How to spot "skimmers" at the grocery store and why you should change your PIN often.
* **The "Too Good to be True" Job:** Spotting fake work-from-home offers that ask for "equipment fees" or your bank login.
* **Safe Search for Housing:** How to avoid rental scams on Craigslist or Facebook where people "rent" houses they don't own.
* **Predatory Loan Apps:** Recognizing "Quick Cash" apps that steal your contacts and harass you for high-interest payments.
* **Medical Debt Scams:** What to do when you get a fake call about an "overdue hospital bill" you don't recognize.
* **Public Wi-Fi Banking:** How to safely check your balance or pay bills if you don’t have home internet and use the library or McDonald's Wi-Fi.
* **Free Phone & Internet Scams:** How to verify real "Lifeline" or government connectivity programs so you don't give your ID to a fake site.
* **"Buy Now, Pay Later" Safety:** Understanding the digital fine print and data sharing of apps like Affirm, Klarna, or Afterpay.
* **Protecting your Tax Refund:** Why you should file early to stop scammers from using your SSN to steal your return.
* **Utility Bill Scams:** What to do if someone calls threatening to "shut off your power" in an hour unless you pay with a gift card.
* **Safe Use of Gig-Economy Apps:** Keeping your Uber, DoorDash, or Instacart driver accounts secure from "verification" scams.
* **Cleaning your Digital Footprint for Jobs:** A quick checklist to make sure your social media doesn't hurt your chances with a new employer.
* **"Free" Gift Card Scams:** Why those "Complete a survey for a $1000 Walmart card" ads are always a data trap.
* **Budget Phone Security:** Simple ways to keep a cheaper or older Android/iPhone safe without buying expensive software.

This list is open for more ideas. If you have a tip that helps someone keep their finances safe during a hard time, we want to hear it!
